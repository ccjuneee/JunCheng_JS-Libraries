About The Museum

VèDA Contemporary Art Museum was founded in 1987 by Elena Vasiliev, a Romanian-born sculptor who immigrated to New Zealand in the late 1970s. After years of exhibiting her own work in makeshift warehouse spaces across Auckland, Elena grew frustrated by the lack of a dedicated venue for radical, non-commercial art in the city.

With a modest bank loan, a donated building on Gallery Lane, and the volunteer labour of seventeen local artists, she opened VèDA's doors on 14 March 1987. The name VèDA — derived from the Sanskrit word for knowledge — reflected her conviction that art and learning were inseparable.

Today, VèDA's permanent collection spans over 4,200 works across painting, sculpture, digital installation, and performance documentation. Our rotating programme hosts 18–24 exhibitions per year, drawing from both established international names and emerging Pacific Rim artists through our residency programme.

Elena passed away in 2019, but her founding principle endures: “Art is not a luxury. It is the record of what it meant to be alive.”
